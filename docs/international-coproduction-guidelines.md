# International Co-Production Guidelines
**Lumina Stream Studios | Business Affairs & Global Productions**
**Version 2.0 | Effective March 2025**

---

## Overview

Lumina Stream Studios produces content across 14 countries and regularly enters into co-production agreements with international partners. This document governs how co-productions are structured, financed, managed, and delivered.

---

## What Is a Co-Production?

A co-production is a formal agreement between Lumina and one or more international production companies (co-producers) to jointly develop, finance, produce, and/or distribute a project. Benefits include:

- Shared production costs and financial risk
- Access to international tax incentives and grants
- Eligibility for local content quotas on foreign platforms
- Creative diversity and authentic local storytelling

---

## Types of Co-Productions

### 1. Treaty Co-Productions
Governed by a bilateral co-production treaty between two countries (e.g., Canada-UK, Australia-Germany). These require certification by the relevant national film agencies (e.g., Telefilm Canada, BFI, Screen Australia).

Requirements:
- Minimum creative and financial contribution thresholds per country (typically 20–80% range per treaty)
- Cast, crew, and spend must meet local content requirements
- Certification must be obtained **before principal photography begins**

Consult BA and the relevant local agency early — certification timelines can be **3–6 months**.

### 2. Non-Treaty Co-Productions
No treaty framework applies; governed entirely by the Co-Production Agreement negotiated between the parties. More flexible but does not confer treaty benefits (e.g., co-production certification, treaty-specific grants).

### 3. Service Productions
Lumina acts as the service company; a foreign producer is the lead. Or vice versa. Not a true co-production — the service company typically has no creative rights or distribution stake.

## Initiating a Co-Production

### Step 1: Business Affairs Engagement
Before any discussions with a potential co-producer:
- Notify Business Affairs (businessaffairs@luminastreamstudios.com)
- BA will assess treaty eligibility, territory rights conflicts, and existing output deals that may affect the project

### Step 2: Term Sheet / LOI
A non-binding Letter of Intent (LOI) is typically signed first, covering:
- Project title and logline
- Each party's financial contribution (%)
- Rights split and territorial allocation
- Intended platforms / distribution strategy
- Preferred delivery timeline

### Step 3: Co-Production Agreement
Long-form agreement executed by both parties before commencement of any paid development or production activity. Key provisions:

| Term | Description |
|---|---|
| Financial Contributions | Each party's share of the budget and payment schedule |
| Creative Control | Who has final cut? How are creative disputes resolved? |
| IP Ownership | Typically proportional to financial contribution |
| Territorial Rights | Which party controls rights in which territories/platforms |
| Credit Requirements | How each party's credit appears on-screen and in marketing |
| Delivery Obligations | Who delivers what to whom and on what timeline |
| Force Majeure | Production disruptions, strikes, acts of God |
| Governing Law | Which country's law governs the agreement |

## Finance & Tax Incentives

### Tracking Co-Production Finance
- Co-production budget is maintained by the Line Producer in coordination with the Lumina Finance team
- International partner contributions are tracked in the **Co-Production Finance Tracker** (Box: `/Legal & Business Affairs/Co-Production Agreements/[PARTNER]/Finance/`)
- Currency exchange risk is managed by Lumina Finance; hedging strategies applied for contributions exceeding USD $500,000

### Tax Incentives & Grants
Each territory has different incentive programs. Common programs Lumina utilizes:

| Territory | Program | Type | Value (approx.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| UK | High-End TV Tax Relief (HETV) | Tax credit | 25% of qualifying spend |
| Canada | Canadian Film/Video Production Tax Credit | Tax credit | 25% of qualifying Canadian labour |
| Australia | Location Offset + PDV Offset | Tax credit | 16.5–30% depending on structure |
| Germany | DFFF (German Federal Film Fund) | Grant | Up to 25% of German spend |
| France | Crédit d'Impôt International | Tax rebate | 30% of qualifying French spend |

Consult BA and Lumina's international tax counsel before budgeting tax incentives — qualification criteria change annually.

## Systems & Data Sharing with Co-Producers

### Google Drive
Co-production partners receive access to a dedicated **shared drive** provisioned by Production Operations:
- Structure mirrors Lumina's standard folder hierarchy
- Partner access level: Viewer for scripts, call sheets, and schedules; Editor for joint deliverables
- Shared drives must be approved by IT and BA before provisioning
- No Lumina proprietary internal documents (budgets, talent rates, HR data) in shared drives

### Confluence (Documentation)
A dedicated Confluence Space (`COPROD-[PARTNER-CODE]`) is created for technical and production documentation shared with partners:
- Post-production technical specs
- Delivery requirements from each territory
- Joint pipeline guides

### Box (Contracts)
Co-production agreement documents are in Box under `/Legal & Business Affairs/Co-Production Agreements/`. Co-producer's legal team is granted Viewer access to the final executed agreement folder only.

### Communication
- Primary: A dedicated Slack Connect channel (provisioned by IT after BA approval)
- Formal: Outlook email for all legally significant communications
- Avoid WhatsApp, iMessage, or personal email for production-related matters

## Credit Requirements

Credits on Lumina co-productions must reflect each party's role:

- **Lumina credit always appears**: "A Lumina Stream Studios Production" (first or second position depending on financial split)
- Co-producer credit: "In co-production with [Partner Name]" or "A [Partner Name] / Lumina Stream Studios Co-Production"
- Co-production fund/agency credits (e.g., "With the participation of Telefilm Canada") as required by treaty/incentive terms
- Credit order and prominence are specified in the Co-Production Agreement

Any deviation from agreed credits requires written approval from both parties' Business Affairs teams.
