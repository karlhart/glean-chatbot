# Legal, Contracts & Talent Buyout Policy
**Lumina Stream Studios | Legal & Business Affairs**
**Version 4.0 | Effective February 2025**

---

## Overview

All contracts, talent agreements, and rights documentation are managed by the Legal & Business Affairs (BA) team and stored in **Box**. This document covers how to initiate, track, execute, and store agreements, as well as the talent buyout process.

---

## Box: Our Contract Repository

### Why Box?
Box is the authoritative system of record for all legal documents at Lumina. It provides:
- Version-controlled contract storage
- DocuSign integration for e-signature workflows
- Granular permissions (not inherited from Google Drive)
- Compliant audit trails for legal and financial review

### Folder Structure in Box

```
/Legal & Business Affairs/
  ├── Talent Agreements/
  │     ├── [YEAR]/
  │     │     ├── Above-the-Line/ (Directors, Showrunners, Lead Cast)
  │     │     └── Below-the-Line/ (Crew, Day Players)
  ├── Co-Production Agreements/
  │     └── [PARTNER-NAME]-[YEAR]/
  ├── Distribution & Licensing/
  ├── Vendor & Service Agreements/
  ├── Music & Clearances/
  └── Insurance & Bonds/
```

### Access
- Box access is requested via IT Help Desk
- Default access is **Viewer** only
- Collaborators (e.g., Line Producers) may be granted **Editor** on their production folder by BA
- External counsel access is provisioned per-engagement by the General Counsel's office

## Initiating a Contract

### Step 1: Submit a Deal Request
All new agreements begin with a **Deal Memo Request** submitted to businessaffairs@luminastreamstudios.com or via the BA intake form in Jira (project: `BA-INTAKE`).

Include:
- Show/project name and code
- Counterparty name and contact
- Deal type (talent, vendor, co-production, etc.)
- Key deal points (rate, term, territory, options)
- Desired execution date

### Step 2: BA Review & Template Selection
Business Affairs will select the appropriate template:
- **Long-form Agreement**: used for above-the-line talent, co-productions, and distribution deals
- **Deal Memo**: used for below-the-line crew and short-term vendor engagements
- **Letter of Intent (LOI)**: used for early-stage co-production or distribution discussions

Typical turnaround: **3–5 business days** for standard agreements; **10+ days** for complex negotiations.

### Step 3: Negotiation & Redlines
- Redlines are tracked in Box with version history
- All counterparty communications go through the assigned BA representative
- Department heads must not negotiate directly with talent or vendors without BA involvement

### Step 4: Execution
- E-signature via DocuSign (integrated into Box)
- Fully executed agreement uploaded to the appropriate Box folder within 24 hours of final signature
- BA notifies the requesting department and Finance upon execution

## Talent Buyout Process

### What Is a Buyout?
A buyout is a one-time payment that covers all future exploitation rights for a performance or creative contribution, in lieu of residual payments. Buyouts are common for:
- Background artists (extras) in non-union productions
- International talent covered under local collective agreements
- Supplemental use rights (e.g., promo, streaming, international)

### When to Request a Buyout
- During casting, if the production intends to use a talent performance across multiple markets or platforms
- When acquiring archival footage or licensed performances
- At the direction of the Showrunner or EP when residual cost projections exceed buyout thresholds

### Buyout Request Process

1. **Production** submits a Buyout Request Form to BA (available in Box: `/Legal & Business Affairs/Templates/`)
2. **BA calculates** the buyout fee based on:
   - Original deal terms and residual formula
   - Number of markets and platforms
   - Union/guild requirements (SAG-AFTRA, DGA, WGA, IATSE)
3. **Finance approval** required for buyouts exceeding **$25,000**
4. **BA drafts** the buyout agreement and routes for signature
5. **Payroll** processes the buyout payment per the executed agreement

### Important Guild & Union Rules

| Guild | Buyout Permitted? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| SAG-AFTRA | Limited | Streaming buyouts allowed for New Media; restricted for theatrical |
| DGA | No | Directors receive residuals; no buyout permitted |
| WGA | No | Writers receive residuals; no buyout permitted |
| IATSE | Case-by-case | Depends on local and basic agreement |
| Non-union | Yes | Buyout terms fully negotiable |

**Always consult BA before promising or offering a buyout to talent or their representatives.**

## Music & Clearances

All music used in Lumina productions — whether pre-existing recordings, compositions, or commissioned scores — requires clearance:

- **Sync License**: Rights to use a composition in the production
- **Master License**: Rights to use a specific recording
- **Work-for-Hire**: Commissioned original music (composer retains no rights)

Music clearance requests are handled by the Music Supervisor in coordination with BA. Lead time: **2–4 weeks** for standard tracks; **4–8 weeks** for popular or complex tracks.

## Contract Renewal & Expiration Tracking

BA maintains a **Contract Expiration Tracker** (Box: `/Legal & Business Affairs/Contract Tracker.xlsx`) updated monthly.

Approaching renewals are flagged to the relevant department head **90 days before expiration**.

## Confidentiality

All contracts and deal terms are **strictly confidential**. Sharing deal terms with anyone outside the authorized access list — including other Lumina employees not party to the agreement — is a violation of company policy and may constitute a breach of contract.
